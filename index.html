Section 2–4

Scope & Policy Requirements

What this policy covers, what it excludes, and the mandatory requirements that must be fulfilled.

✅

In Scope

  • All partner resources onboarded via Purchase Request (PR)
  • PR Team activities post-budget approval
  • Logistics Team – ORF & RMG submissions
  • IT Team – Hardware & system access provisioning
  • RMG – Resource allocation & Clarity setup
  • Project Managers – Deployment coordination
  • Resource Managers (DU/PU Lead) – Oversight
❌

Out of Scope

  • Agreement covering Code of Conduct
  • Email creation
  • Asset allocation
  • ID Card issuance
  • Biometrics
  • Workstation allocation
  • Post-onboarding procedures
📜

Policy Requirements

4.1
Same-Day Initiation The PR Team shall initiate onboarding activities on the same working day of PR budget approval or receipt of PR details from the CIS Team.
4.2
Minimum Onboarding Checklist Initiation must include: new joiner request, Clarity account, hardware & IT account, RMG request, PM & Resource Manager induction coordination, Resource Database, PR tracker, and Induction Tracker updates.
4.3
Complete ORF Submission Logistics Team shall submit complete ORF and allocation details to RMG, extracted from the CIF or approved PR documentation. PMs consulted only for discrepancies.
4.4
Task Creation within 1 Working Day Tasks must be created within 1 working day of account creation. For urgent onboarding, tasks must be created the same day.
4.5
Induction Before DOJ All induction activities must be completed on or before the resource's joining date to ensure operational readiness and compliance.
Section 5

Team Procedures

Step-by-step responsibilities for each team involved in the onboarding process. Select a team below to view their tasks.

🗂 PR Team

Initiates and coordinates all onboarding activities upon PR approval or CIS receipt

1
Submit TAMM request for hardware provisioning, IT account, and required system access (VPN, jump server, etc.) TAMM
2
Share resource details with SBU Operations to update the Resource Database.
3
Provide resource details to Logistics Team to initiate RMG account setup.
4
Coordinate with Logistics Team to ensure required induction activities are arranged for new joiners.
5
Ensure the NDA is issued to the resource on the same working day where required by the IT Team. Same Day

🚚 Logistics Team

Manages RMG setup, induction coordination, tracker updates, and Day-1 readiness

1
Submit complete RMG request and ensure resource setup is completed in Clarity.
2
Extract and provide required resource deployment details from the approved CIF. Contact PM only when information is missing or unclear.
  • Resource Name, Delivery Unit Name, Project Name & Code
  • ORF Role Name, Start/End Date, Line Code, Allocation %
3
Send the welcome email in the pre-defined format once the IT account is activated.
4
Coordinate the induction process with IT, RMG, PM, and Resource Manager.
5
Confirm tasks are assigned to the resource to enable work commencement and timesheet recording.
6
Update the Induction Tracker to reflect onboarding status and completion.
7
Coordinate with Resource Manager to confirm approval for Udemy access and request from L&D team.
8
Raise a workstation request based on Resource Manager's requirements.
9
Confirm Day-1 readiness with the resource and escalate any access or setup issues promptly. Day 1
10
Maintain visibility of pending onboarding items and ensure closure within defined timelines.
11
Provide briefing to new joiners on timesheet submission: timelines, approval workflow, and compliance expectations.
12
Inform Resource Manager and update trackers upon notification of delays, cancellations, or deployment changes.

💻 IT Team

Responsible for hardware, access provisioning, and biometric activation

1
Provision and issue laptop and required IT assets/accessories as part of IT induction, prior to the resource's joining date where applicable. Before DOJ
2
Activate the resource's domain access and create the official email ID before the resource commences work at the client site.
3
Activate biometric access on the day of joining, or the first day the resource reports physically to the office (for virtual onboarding). Day 1

🏢 General Services

Handles workspace allocation and access support items

1
Allocate appropriate workspace for the new hire prior to or on the day of joining. Before DOJ
2
Issue required office access and support items — ID card (temporary/permanent), parking sticker (if applicable), essential stationery — within 2 working days of joining. ≤ 2 Days

📊 EPMO / PMO

Manages Clarity access, timesheet setup, and first-month compliance monitoring

1
Activate Clarity access for new hires within 2 working days from DOJ (Delivery Team resources only). ≤ 2 Days
2
Assign the relevant project and reporting manager in Clarity to enable task allocation and timesheet approvals.
3
Share Clarity usage guidelines and timesheet submission procedures with the new joiner within 2 working days.
4
Provide initial support to the new joiner for timesheet submission and resolve access or task allocation issues promptly.
5
Monitor timesheet submission compliance during the first month of onboarding and follow up on non-submissions. Month 1

📁 Project Manager

Confirms deployment details, ensures Clarity setup, and briefs the resource on Day 1

1
Confirm resource deployment details in line with the approved CIF, project plan, and ORF.
2
Provide any missing or clarifying deployment details requested by Logistics or RMG in a timely manner.
3
Ensure resource is allocated in Clarity with the correct project, role, and allocation percentage.
4
Ensure project tasks are created and assigned in Clarity for work commencement and timesheet recording.
5
Brief the resource on project context, immediate responsibilities, reporting structure, and priorities on or before Day 1. Day 1
6
Notify RMG and Logistics promptly if onboarding is delayed, cancelled, or deployment details change.

👔 Resource Manager (DU/PU Lead)

Validates alignment, approves allocation, and supports deployment readiness

1
Confirm resource deployment requirements in alignment with the approved CIF and delivery plan.
2
Validate role alignment, allocation percentage, and deployment duration prior to onboarding.
3
Review and approve the resource allocation request submitted to RMG.
4
Confirm workstation, access, or any special setup requirements to Logistics, where applicable.
5
Approve training, certifications, and tool access required for the resource to perform responsibilities.
6
Support resolution of onboarding readiness issues that may impact deployment timelines.
7
Manage allocation and utilization upon notification of delays, cancellations, or deployment changes.
8
Provide overview of DU/PU structure including reporting lines, key contacts, and escalation pathways relevant to the resource's role.

Sections 7–9

Exceptions, Risks & Non-Compliance

How to handle exceptions, what governance risks this policy mitigates, and consequences of non-compliance.

⚡

Exception Scenarios

🚨 Urgent Onboarding Required

Expedited approval granted. All compliance steps completed retrospectively within defined timelines.

📊 Clarity / Task Allocation Delay

PMO and PM must resolve within 1 working day of Date of Joining.

💻 IT Provisioning Delay

Logistics Team to escalate immediately to IT Management.

📄 Incomplete Onboarding Documentation

Must be regularized within 2 working days.

⚖️ Compliance / Billing / Audit Impact

Escalate immediately to relevant BU Heads & SBU GRC.

🚫

Non-Compliance Definition

Failure to adhere to this policy may result in operational disruption, financial impact, or compliance exposure. Non-compliance includes:

  • Delayed onboarding initiation after PR approval
  • Failure to provide system access or tasks within defined timelines
  • Incomplete onboarding documentation or tracker updates
  • Resource deployment without Clarity setup or allocation
  • Failure to complete induction requirements
  • Delays in communicating onboarding changes
⚠️ Repeated or material non-compliance may be escalated to SBU leadership and relevant governance functions for corrective action.
Section 10

Escalation & Corrective Action

A four-level escalation framework to address onboarding delays, process deviations, and governance risks.

⚡ Escalation Triggers

SLA timelines not met
Day-1 readiness at risk
System access not completed
Incomplete documentation
Cross-functional dependency delays
Repeated non-compliance
Level 1
Immediate Issue
Operational Resolution
The responsible team attempts immediate resolution upon identifying the delay. Coordinate with relevant stakeholders to close pending items without delay.
Level 2
SLA Breach
Functional Escalation
If not resolved within defined SLA, escalate to the respective function lead (IT Manager, PMO Head, Finance Head, Resource Manager, or Delivery Lead). Provide status, impact, and expected resolution timeline.
Level 3
Deployment / Billing Risk
SBU Operations Escalation
If delays may impact deployment timelines, billing readiness, or compliance, escalate to the SBU Operations Lead for cross-functional intervention and resolution.
Level 4
Critical Risk
Management Escalation
For critical risks or repeated delays, escalate to SBU Leadership for immediate corrective direction and organizational accountability.
